# Semiconductor Physics: Complete Introduction

## 1. What is a Semiconductor?

A semiconductor is a material whose electrical conductivity lies between a conductor and an insulator. The conductivity of a semiconductor can be controlled by temperature, impurities, and electric fields.

Examples of semiconductors:

* Silicon (Si)
* Germanium (Ge)
* Gallium Arsenide (GaAs)

Semiconductors are the foundation of modern electronics such as diodes, transistors, integrated circuits, and solar cells.

---

## 2. Energy Band Structure

In solids, electrons occupy energy bands:

* Valence Band → electrons bound to atoms
* Conduction Band → free electrons move and conduct current
* Band Gap → energy difference between valence and conduction band

### Types of Materials Based on Band Gap

Conductor

* Valence and conduction band overlap
* Very high conductivity

Semiconductor

* Small band gap (≈ 0.1 eV – 3 eV)
* Moderate conductivity

Insulator

* Large band gap (> 5 eV)
* Very low conductivity

Example:

* Silicon band gap = 1.1 eV
* Germanium band gap = 0.7 eV

---

## 3. Intrinsic Semiconductor

A pure semiconductor without impurities is called intrinsic semiconductor.

Characteristics:

* Equal electrons and holes
* Low conductivity
* Conductivity increases with temperature

When temperature increases:

* Electrons jump to conduction band
* Holes created in valence band
* Current flows

Electron = negative charge carrier
Hole = positive charge carrier

Thus, conduction occurs due to both electrons and holes.

---

## 4. Extrinsic Semiconductor

When impurity is added to intrinsic semiconductor, it becomes extrinsic semiconductor.

Two types:

### n-type Semiconductor

Pentavalent impurity added:

* Phosphorus
* Arsenic
* Antimony

These atoms donate extra electron.

Majority carrier = electrons
Minority carrier = holes

Example:
Si + Phosphorus → n-type semiconductor

---

### p-type Semiconductor

Trivalent impurity added:

* Boron
* Aluminium
* Gallium

These create holes.

Majority carrier = holes
Minority carrier = electrons

Example:
Si + Boron → p-type semiconductor

---

## 5. Fermi Level

Fermi level represents probability of electron occupancy.

Intrinsic semiconductor:

* Fermi level at middle of band gap

n-type semiconductor:

* Fermi level moves toward conduction band

p-type semiconductor:

* Fermi level moves toward valence band

This helps determine electrical behavior.

---

## 6. Charge Carriers

Two charge carriers exist:

Electrons

* Negative charge
* Move in conduction band

Holes

* Positive charge
* Move in valence band

Total current:

Current = electron current + hole current

---

## 7. Drift Current

When electric field applied:

* Electrons move opposite to field
* Holes move in field direction

This produces drift current.

Drift current depends on:

* Electric field
* Mobility
* Carrier concentration

---

## 8. Diffusion Current

When carrier concentration differs:

* Electrons move high → low concentration
* Holes move high → low concentration

This produces diffusion current.

Diffusion occurs even without external field.

---

## 9. Conductivity of Semiconductor

Conductivity depends on:

* Number of electrons
* Number of holes
* Mobility

Conductivity increases with:

* Temperature
* Doping

Unlike metals, semiconductor conductivity increases with temperature.

---

## 10. Applications of Semiconductor

Semiconductors are used in:

* Diodes
* Transistors
* Solar cells
* Integrated circuits
* LEDs
* Sensors
* Amplifiers

Modern electronics are based on semiconductor devices.

---

## Summary

A semiconductor is a material with moderate conductivity.
It has small band gap.
Conduction occurs due to electrons and holes.
Doping produces n-type and p-type semiconductors.
Semiconductors are used in electronic devices.
